About

Data engineer. Analyst. Writer.

I work at the intersection of data systems and clear communication. That sounds like a brand statement. It’s actually just what happened.

I have a background in computer engineering and I’m finishing a master’s in Information Management with a concentration in finance and analytics at the University of Illinois. I’ve built pipelines, automated test suites, e-commerce platforms, and BI dashboards across industries that don’t obviously connect: fintech, optics retail, QA, and newsrooms.

I also write. Not as a side hobby. I spent three years as a Senior Editor at Mid Day, one of Mumbai’s major daily newspapers. I’ve published cultural essays, science communication for local government, and interview profiles for a UN-affiliated organization. The writing and the technical work don’t compete. They come from the same habit: look at how something works, find what’s not working, and fix it in the clearest way available.

What I care about

  • I don’t ship things I wouldn’t use myself.
  • Clarity beats cleverness, every time.
  • The best solution is usually the simpler one.

What people hire me for

  • Building data pipelines and dashboards that actually get used, not just built.
  • Translating technical work into something non-technical stakeholders can act on.
  • Finding the gap between what the data says and what needs to change.
Currently

I’m in Champaign, IL, finishing the last courses of my MS and preparing for what’s next. Previously worked on a Digital Accessibility project at UIUC’s Digital Risk Office. Open to full-time roles starting mid-2026 in data engineering, analytics engineering, or adjacent roles where both technical and communication skills are useful.

I work well in small teams. I don’t need everything to be defined before I start.
