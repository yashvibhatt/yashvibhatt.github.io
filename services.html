Services

What I Can Do For You

Four services. Each one is practical, scoped, and built around your actual problem, not a standard package.

01

Data Pipeline Design & Engineering

For teams sitting on data they can’t use. Analysts spending their Mondays cleaning spreadsheets. Companies where “the data is in there somewhere” is a common phrase.

What I deliver
  • ETL pipeline design and build (Python, Apache NiFi)
  • Database schema design and optimization (MySQL, PostgreSQL, Oracle)
  • Data source integration APIs, flat files, databases
  • Automated validation and cleaning logic
  • Documentation your next engineer can actually follow
Problems it solves
  • Data scattered across five systems with no clean way to combine it
  • Manual data prep eating analyst time every week
  • Pipelines that work but nobody understands anymore
  • Reporting that’s always a day behind
Proof

A financial data pipeline I built cut manual data prep effort by 40% and brought Tableau dashboards from next-day to near real-time. See the case study →

02

BI Dashboards & Analytics

For business teams who have data and need to see it clearly. Managers making decisions off gut instinct. Analysts who know what questions to ask but can’t surface the answers fast enough.

What I deliver
  • Dashboard design and build (Power BI, Tableau)
  • SQL query optimization for faster load times
  • Metric framework design figuring out which numbers actually matter
  • Training so your team can use it without calling me
Problems it solves
  • Dashboards that take three minutes to load
  • Reports that exist but nobody trusts
  • Metrics tracked because they’ve always been tracked, not because they’re useful
  • Data that tells you what happened, but not why
Proof

Rebuilt Power BI dashboards at Sapient Wealth Advisors with automated refresh and optimized SQL. Reports went from requiring manual updates to staying current on their own. See the case study →

QA & Test Automation

For engineering teams releasing software on tight cycles. QA teams drowning in manual regression. Product teams who’ve been burned by defects that should have been caught.

  • Manual testing eating release cycle time

  • QA bottlenecking the whole team
